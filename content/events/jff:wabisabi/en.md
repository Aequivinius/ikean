Traditional Japanese Tea Ceremony at [Japan Food Fest](https://www.japanfoodfest.ch/)

With trained tea practitioner [Nico Čolić](https://www.ikean.ch), you will receive a fascinating one-hour introduction to the world of the Japanese tea ceremony. In an approachable yet respectful way, Nico shares insights into the most prominent tea school (裏千家, *urasenke*), offering guests a rare opportunity to experience this refined cultural tradition.

During the presentation, each participant receive a handmade seasonal sweet and a bowl of tea imported directly from Japan from 丸久小山園 (*marukyū koyamaen*, one of the few tea producers officially appointed by the Grand Master), allowing you to experience the ceremony through taste, atmosphere, and explanation.

The topic of this presentation will be: **The *wabi sabi* of Tea.** This *austere beauty* is an aesthetic and philosophical concept that strongly shapes the tea ceremony. Although it has become popular again today, it is often misunderstood or oversimplified. With a handmade bowl in your hands, the slightly astringent taste of tea on your palate, and a historically informed explanation, it becomes much easier to grasp this elusive concept.

This presentation will be held in German both on the 3.5. and the 5.5 at 16:30.
